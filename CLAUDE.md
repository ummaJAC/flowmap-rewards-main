# GeoCorp: Real-World Web3 Monopoly 🏙️

## Project Goal
Build a location-based GameFi/DePIN mobile web app ("GeoCorp") where players capture real-world physical businesses, mint them as NFTs on the Flow EVM blockchain, and earn passive yield. It’s a mix of Monopoly, Pokémon GO, and Foursquare.

## Core Loop
1. **Explore:** User opens map, sees unowned businesses (pulled from OpenStreetMap Overpass API).
2. **Capture (Proof of Physical Presence):** User walks to the coordinates, takes a photo of the storefront.
3. **AI Verification:** OpenRouter GPT-4o-mini verifies the image is actually a storefront, not a picture of a monitor or a wall.
4. **Permanent Storage:** The verified photo is uploaded to IPFS via Storacha.
5. **Mint NFT:** The business is minted as a unique NFT on Flow EVM Testnet, assigned to the user.
6. **Flex & Earn:** A 3D model pops up on the map at those coordinates. The user earns passive "GeoPoints" (GP) per hour, which can be used to buy other properties on the Marketplace.

## Tech Stack
*   **Frontend:** React, TypeScript, Vite, Tailwind CSS, Mapbox GL JS (with 3D models)
*   **AI Oracle:** Express.js Backend + OpenRouter (GPT-4o-Mini) for visual validation
*   **Blockchain:** Flow EVM Testnet (Solidity SMART CONTRACT - ERC721 NFT + Yield generation)
*   **Storage:** Storacha Network (IPFS) for immutable photo proofs

## Monetization Strategy (The "Trojan Horse" 3-Stage Model)
*Targeting early-stage (<10k users) survival without relying on impossible B2B data sales.*

### Stage 1: The Bootstrap (0 - 1,000 Users)
Funded by grants/VCs. The goal is pure DAU growth, but we generate baseline revenue via:
1. **In-App Purchases (Energy & Radars):** "Free-to-play" limits users to 3 captures a day. Users buy "Golden Film" (Energy) or "Radar Boosts" (reveal hidden spots) via Flow/Credit Card to play longer.
2. **Utility Features (Payload Methods):**
   - **Asset Reservation:** User sees an unowned POI on the map some distance away. They pay $1 to "Lock/Reserve" it for 24 hours so no one else can capture it while they travel there physically.
   - **Auto-Claim Managers (Subscription):** Yield must be claimed manually every 24h. Whales with 50+ properties will gladly pay a $10/mo subscription for an "AI Manager" that auto-claims and repairs their entire portfolio.
3. **Franchise Modules (Yield Boosters):** Users can buy "Modules" from the in-app shop (e.g., "Golden Signboard" for $5). Attaching this to their NFT property permanently boosts its daily yield by +20% and changes its 3D model look. Pure utility sink for optimizers.
4. **The "Legendary 1% Mint" (Generative Rarity):** When a user captures a business, they don't just get a marker—they mint it. There is a programmed **1% chance that the AI assigns a 'Legendary' 3D model** to it. A Legendary NFT has a permanent x2 or x3 yield multiplier. This creates an insane psychological drive to hunt/capture, and forms a hyper-liquid secondary market where players buy/sell Legendary businesses from each other inside GeoCorp.

## AI Oracle Architecture 2.0 (Reference-Based Verification)
Relying solely on zero-shot vision (Option A) is unstable for local businesses that lack famous logos or explicit text signs. To achieve a 99% stable verification rate, GeoCorp uses **Reference-Based AI Verification**:

1. **The Core Problem:** A generic cafe without a huge neon sign will get falsely rejected by standard AI because the AI doesn't know what that specific cafe looks like.
2. **The Reference Solution:**
   - When a user captures a location `[lat, lng]`, the backend queries the **Google Places API** or **Google Street View Static API**.
   - The backend downloads the official "Reference Image" of that specific business from Google Maps.
   - The backend sends **TWO** images to GPT-4o-mini Vision: 
     1) The User's Camera Photo
     2) The Google Maps Reference Photo
   - **The Prompt:** *"Compare Image A (User) with Image B (Google Reference). Do they depict the exact same storefront/building exterior? Are the architectural details, window placements, or signs matching?"*
3. **Economics:** This doubles the image token cost per validation (from ~$0.002 to ~$0.004) and requires Google API calls, but it guarantees high stability and solves the "No-Name Local Business" problem. This is a massive selling point for the hackathon judges (proving we thought through real-world edge cases).
